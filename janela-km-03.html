First of all, let’s be clear here: although the visit is very important, it is not a "to go" or "not to go" factor.
Is it more about getting extra credits for showing serious interest in the program and in the overall process?
Sure. Besides doing your homework to be seen as a serious candidate, you can approach other few concrete elements that should make you more competitive.
  • You can explore in your essays and interview the insights collected during your trip;
  • You may narrow down your list of choices after visiting different schools;
  • Upon returning, you will probably have a personal ranking of desired MBA programs. Thus, you may also change the list of school to which you had originally considered applying. Think about the money and time you can save;
  • In the case of a waitlist, it is practically mandatory that you pay a visit to reinforce your interest in the School; thus, being there before will save you the work;
  • If you are waitlisted in December, you will be better positioned to manage the situation. You will have established contact with current MBA students, AdCom officers, interviewers, etc. It is wise for waitlisted applicants to show interest in the program by visiting the school in February; it is a reactive move, though;
  • In November, the interviewer will probably appreciate knowing that you have visited his/her school. You will better articulate your answer to the Why-School question in your essay and interview;
  • In case of multiple admissions, you will be in good position to choose;
  • You will expand your network - during your visit you may even meet the guy who will play a decisive role in your candidacy;
  • Last but not least: consider visiting the school during the MBA application process; after all, you are planning to spend the next two years of your life in that environment. You should know it in advance.

But, Megaron, when is the best time to visit?
Undoubtedly, you should visit the school before you submit your application, but, naturally, check whether students or AdCom members are on campus.
I understand I should visit schools when I can enjoy all four on this list: Class Visit, Campus Tour, Meeting AdCom and Lunch, right?
Exactly. Classes often happen from SEPTEMBER to MAY. The first-years will arrive around July-August, but they are not the most indicated to reveal insights into the school. In fact, they may be just as lost as you.
Any additional advice?
Pay attention to: (i.) spring break and others; (ii.) local holidays; and (iii.) weekdays with no classes (usually Fridays). You don’t want to fly all the way there to find an empty campus. We suggest two "best moments":
    01.) Before submitting your application
    02.) Just after you are invited to an interview
    These points emphasize the benefits of an on campus interview:
  • AdCom members (sometimes second-year students) will interview you. Remember that they are actual "decision makers";
  • You will be seen as a candidate with genuine interest in the School;
  • You will be in a good position if you are eventually placed on a "Wait List"; an interview with an AdCom officer will give you the chance of exchanging e-mails with a "decision maker" who is well acquainted with your case.
Do I need to schedule this kind of event?
Although many schools offer ‘Drop in Sessions’, we recommend that you maximize your chances of enjoying your visit fully. You should have a defined schedule two weeks before you depart.
Should I wear a three-piece suit?
Be sensible: have you scheduled anything formal with an AdCom member? In this case, dress formally. Otherwise, a clean, ironed shirt and a neat jacket will do.
Is one week enough?
In general, one day per school should do. If you’re not going to the West coast, then one week is enough.
