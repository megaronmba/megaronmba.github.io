What is a Résumé?

This one-page document, the first that Admissions Committee Officers will read, works as a guide, a reference point for them. Through your résumé, they can measure the duration of your jobs, evolution of responsibilities, progression of salaries, and achievements/results.

This document is a piece of advertisement – a selling tool that summarizes your skills, experiences, and achievements.

The résumé can free up space in your essays, so that you can be more objective in your stories.



Résumé - Online Application Form - Essays

Your résumé should not contradict information from the online application data form. Likewise, essays and résumé should be complementary to sharpen your message and offer your application depth and breadth.

Consistent information prevents confusion and keeps unity and coherence in your application. You must refer to companies, job titles, departments, technologies, and other items in the same way in both résumé and essays.

Keep in mind you will be sending a package of documents to each B-school. Elaborate your résumé now, but review it again when you are done writing your essays.



What is an Effective Résumé Like?

A résumé should:
  • Be a succinct presentation of yourself;
  • Work as a strategic piece to emphasize teamwork, analysis, leadership, and management;
  • Function as a marketing tool that distinguishes you from other applicants;
  • Have no more than one page with organized and formatted information;
  • Focus on achievements rather than just activities;
  • Present no grammar mistakes; confusing ideas; incorrect information.
