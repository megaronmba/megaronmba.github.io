OK, then. Could you walk me through the MBA Bureaucracy? What should I know now, six months before application deadlines?
Most MBA schools ask for 3 documents that provide them with some evidence of your academic potential: School Transcripts, Diploma, and Grading System. You should obtain these documents now in your college’s registrar (‘secretaria’). It is easy. You can e-mail most schools and request these documents. Please, do it now.
I am ok with the first two documents, but what is a Grading System?
It is a letter to explain how your school grades work.
Should I send these documents together with my application package?
These 3 documents will be used in two different moments: (i.) during the application process and (ii.) upon enrollment. (Important: not all business schools will ask for all three documents listed above - you must check into each school’s website for details).
Sorry, I am not sure I get it. Why “two moments”?
Most MBA schools will ask you for those documents when you will enroll their programs.
Huh? You mean I do not have to send a document (hard copy) when I submit my application?
Exactly, most MBA Schools do not require any hard copy during the application phase. Nevertheless, be careful: check which schools still request official transcripts in hard copy (some will request them even before admission).
Very few schools will demand a hard copy of documents while I am applying, right?
The tendency is to eliminate hard copies, at least until the student enrolls the program.
So, how does it work?
During the application process, most b-schools require that you provide self-report transcripts. Therefore, you will only need one copy of transcripts and its certified translation. You will scan them or copy the content into the website – the action will vary from school to school (sometimes they offer both options: scanned or self-reported information).
It makes sense: they will only check those documents during enrollment time, right?
Upon enrollment, B-schools will request your documents in hard copy (paper). You will have to return to your college or university to have everything sent in one envelope that must be sealed and signed across by the college registrar (someone from your college administration).
And what about Transcripts and Grading System? I know they use the so-called GPA.
Grade Point Average (GPA) is a standard way of measuring academic achievement in the USA. GPA assumes a grading scale of A, B, C, D, F. An A grade receives 4 points… and an F=0.
You will not need to convert any grades - all top-ranked Admission Committee (AdCom) members are familiar with most Brazilian Top Undergraduate Programs (FGV-EAESP, POLI-USP, ITA, FEA-USP, FEI, MAUÁ, PUC-SP, ESPM-SP, UNICAMP, UFRJ, UEERJ, UFRGS, PUC-RS, UFMG, etc.) FGV-EAESP and POLI-USP even offer an explanation on ranking system (“Carta Ranking” or “Atestado de Equivalência”). Examples: FGV-EAESP reveals the student’s quartile. “Your grade is 7.2 and you are among the best 25%”; POLI-USP: “You are ranked 5th in Industrial Engineering (40 students) and 87th at POLI (567 students)”
As far as I know, my college does not have a letter explaining the grade system. How will my college explain my grades?
You can send them a model via e-mail and request them to print, sign, and stamp it. Observe the model below. This is only a reference. Do NOT copy it.

Preferably printed on SCHOOL stationery

City, month, day, year.

SUBJECT: Undergraduate Course Marking System

Scores for the undergraduate courses in SCHOOL NAME are on a decimal scale and therefore cannot simply be converted into the 0-4 range of the traditional GPA from US institutions.

We use a continuous mark grading system from 0.0 to 10.0 where below 5 is a fail. The score is individual. Scores are the result of xxx (on exercises, xx, xx, and exams).

APPLICANT’s Name, who has requested us this letter, is a former student of this school and earned a XX as final grade.

We would like to inform that the xx office will be pleased to provide xxx about the xx, its courses, and xx xx.

Signature of Registrar POSITION
(Stamp if available)

Any doubt?
Sure. Megaron, I‘ve attended an exchange program abroad. Should I get the transcripts from these courses? I have got pretty good grades.
You should inform the grades of all courses in your undergrad program. B-schools will require a scanned copy of the official transcript or an uploaded electronic version provided by the institution. Transcripts for exchange programs or transfer programs are not required if the grades are included on the transcript of the degree-granting institution. Check whether your college will issue the grades on courses taken abroad (exchange program, for example). If not, contact the school you attended abroad.
And what about the diploma? Should I worry about the diploma now? Or will I need it just at enrollment time, next year? What’s the translation job like in this case?”
If you have the time, why not do it now? You should have your diploma copied and certified (autenticado). Then, have it translated into English by a certified translator. Later, upon enrolment, you will have to send the b-school the certified copy and the translated version of your diploma.
Megaron, do I need to send in the original version for translation or can I e-mail the scanned transcripts?
To certify a document in another language, the translator must stamp the original document as proof that the translation job was done from the real document. Ideally, you should leave all original documents with the translator. Alternatively, you can scan the document (make sure it is complete and clear). The translator can check the originals when he or she delivers the translations. Ask the translator you hired about his/her work conditions.
I do not have a clue of the number of schools to which I will apply. How many copies of translated documents I should ask the translator? Since we are at the beginning of the Marathon, I do not want to waste money.
We are sure of at least one thing: you will need a copy of your documents next year in order to enroll your b-school. Request only one copy of translations. The certified translator will charge you a given amount for the first translation job and can provide you with additional copies at 20% of that amount. If you ask for extra copies at a later moment, he or she will normally ask for 50% of the original value charged. Thus, carefully review your list of schools before you make your request. Again, check the schools’ websites. Each school has its own policy on bureaucracy. (Important: observe interview procedures. Some schools may request printed documents.)
